Overview

Health conscious and digitally enabled consumers, are demanding more from the health and wellness industry. The demand is expected to swell the consumer health market by more than $200 Billion between 2013 and 2018 [1]. Concurrent to this tsunami of demand, is the ever-rising level of global pollution. Here, in London, air pollution is ostensibly the most significant public health issue. Research performed by King’s College London estimated that air pollution was responsible for up to 141,000 life years lost or the equivalent of up to 9,400 deaths in London in 2010, as well as over 3,400 hospital admissions [2]. The interwoven swell of demand and rise in pollution has laid bare the chasm between consumer wants and industry provisions. Filling this chasm was the opportunity our team has sought to seize. Freshly was born.

Freshly is a mobile application for monitoring, grading, and analysing an individual's daily and historical exposure to various pollutants, thus empowering users to make health-conscious and informed decisions. As my second native Android application, it again presented an excellent learning experience, especially for working in a larger team comprised of many different roles. Clients from the London Sustainability Exchange went on to adopt the codebase following the app's completion.

Features

  • Analyse location data over the course of a day
  • Integrate with London air quality data
  • Grade the user's exposure to air pollutants
  • Visualise exposure over time

Contribution

  • My first main contribution was the co-leadership of the frontend team, helping them to progress and develop the most cutting edge Android application possible within the short amount of time that was given to us (10 weeks). This includes assigning individual tasks, reporting progress and presenting new ideas to the management team as well as documenting team meetings (on Google Drive) and the overall development progress (Trello). The reason for choosing two team leaders was simply so that one can communicate with the management team and the other with the backend team. Consequently, we were able to focus on the communication with one other sub-team at the time which resulted in a better overall communication amongst the sub-teams which in return prevented misunderstandings.
  • The other substantial contribution was design and implementation of the entire Freshly Android application UI. This was achieved through steady progress every week and constant feedback provided by the other frontend team members and the management team.
