Overview

The purpose of this project was to design and build an auction system (similar to Ebay) as part of the Database and Information Systems COMP3013 module in year 3. My team and I were assigned to use PHP for developing the backend (backend frameworks were prohibited), SQL for the database management, HMTL/CSS/JS/Bootstrap for the frontend of the web application, MAMP as the development environment and Microsoft Azure for deployment.

The project lasted 8 weeks and was entirely self-directed in the sense that the team itself had to interpret on how to achieve set system requirements. In other words, it was my team’s responsibility to adequately translate the requirements into what data should be stored and the transactions that those data should support, how the data will be modified and who will have access to it.

Features

The online auction system we build had the following capabilities:

  • Users can register with the system and create accounts as well as have roles of seller or buyer with different privileges.
  • Sellers can create auctions for particular items, setting suitable conditions and features of the items including the item description, categorisation, starting price, reserve price and end date.
  • Buyers can search the system for particular kinds of item being auctioned and can browse and visually re-arrange listings of items within categories.
  • Buyers can bid for items and see other buyers’ bids. The system will manage the auction until the set end time and award the item to the highest bidder. The system should confirm to both the winner and seller of an auction its outcome.
  • Buyers can watch auctions on items and receive emailed updates on bids on those items including notifications when they are outbid.
  • Sellers can receive reports on the progress of the auction through to completion and how much viewing traffic their auction items have had.
  • Buyers and sellers have visible ratings aggregated from the feedback on their participation in auctions.
  • Buyers can receive recommendations for items to bid on based on collaborative filtering (i.e., users might want to bid on the sorts of things other people, who have also bid on the sorts of things they have previously bid on, are currently bidding on).
  • The database was populated with data to simulate real usage, so that the queries and functionality may be tested, and demonstrated for purposes of the presentation video.

Even though, the presentation design of the user interface and its usability was not being accessed, we spend a considerable amount of time on developing a user-friendly and responsive web application using the Bootstrap framework.
